## Official Choice Advantage / SkyTouch /CONNECT data feed for Staxis — feasibility, cost, process, and contacts (fact-checked June 2026)

## Choice Hotels Brand Standards / Quality Assurance (QA) Review program + Qualified Vendor path — to power a Staxis "QA Evidence Pack" feature

**Bottom line:** Choice enforces brand standards through scheduled AND unannounced on-site QA reviews scoring cleanliness, condition, and brand-standard compliance, plus a parallel guest-satisfaction track (Medallia surveys ~48h after checkout). Properties below a minimum score get re-inspected more often and escalate through written warnings → re-inspection / non-compliance / guest-satisfaction fees → mandatory training → ultimately franchise termination (verbatim from Choice's SEC 10-K). A continuous, timestamped self-audit + evidence pack maps almost 1:1 onto this enforcement ladder. Becoming a "Choice Qualified Vendor" is a real, defined path — a 9-page / 140-field online application, 4-6 week review, gated on national reach, hospitality references, financial stability, and direct-order integration — and software ops tools have gotten in (Quore, 2016), but it is premature until Staxis has multi-property proof.

### Findings
- WHAT IS INSPECTED (CONFIRMED): Choice's QA reviews evaluate property condition, cleanliness, guest-service standards, and brand-standard compliance, monitored via 'scheduled and unannounced quality assurance reviews conducted periodically at each property and through the use of guest surveys.' Coverage mirrors industry QA: guest rooms + bathrooms, public areas/lobby, exterior/grounds, pool, maintenance, and breakfast/F&B. (Choice SEC 10-K, FY2021/FY2023 language is consistent with FY2016)
- HOW OFTEN (CONFIRMED, performance-driven): Reviews are 'scheduled and unannounced' and run periodically — a full QA review is roughly annual, but 'properties that fail to maintain a minimum score are re-inspected on a more frequent basis until deficiencies are cured, or until such properties are terminated.' So cadence escalates with failure, it is not fixed. Choice's own preventive-maintenance brand standard separately requires guest rooms be inspected a minimum of 2 times per year. (SEC 10-K; Choice University 'New Entry Owners' maintenance page)
- SCORING (CONFIRMED as % score; pass line NOT public): Choice uses a percentage score — a real reported example: Comfort Inn Gaithersburg scored 96.4% on an UNANNOUNCED inspection (announced Apr 2018), 'in the top 100 hotels out of 6,600 Choice Hotels worldwide in cleanliness, condition and Brand standards.' The score is set by an impartial/third-party inspector, not the GM. A 'minimum score' threshold exists (the escalation ladder keys off it) but Choice does NOT publish the numeric pass line. (promarkpartners 96.4% case; SEC 10-K)
- GUEST-SATISFACTION TRACK (CONFIRMED, separate but linked): Choice runs guest surveys via Medallia, sent within ~48 hours of checkout, producing GIS (guest-satisfaction) and LTR (likelihood-to-recommend) scores. Choice instructs hotels to review Medallia GIS/LTR daily. Low guest-satisfaction scores feed the same enforcement and can trigger 'guest satisfaction fees.' (Medallia Choice case study; Choice University 'Guest Relations' page; SEC 10-K)
- FAILURE / ENFORCEMENT LADDER (CONFIRMED, verbatim from SEC 10-K): franchisees who fail to improve on identified quality matters 'may be subject to consequences ranging from written warnings, the payment of re-inspection, non-compliance and guest satisfaction fees, attendance at mandatory training programs and ultimately to the termination of the franchise agreement.' Consequences are at Choice's discretion, case-by-case. This exact language appears across multiple 10-K years (FY2016 through FY2021+).
- PIP (Property Improvement Plan) — the capital-renovation cousin of QA: Choice can require franchisees to renovate to current brand specs; missing PIP milestones can carry penalties up to termination. PIP/QA both check that equipment (TVs, PTAC/HVAC, appliances) meets CURRENT commercial brand specs even if the old gear still works. NOTE: the often-cited '600 terminations in a year' (Steve Joyce era) is anecdotal/dated — treat as illustrative, not a current figure. (hospitalitynet 'Dark Side of PIPs'; twstransworld)
- FRANCHISEE PROTECTIONS — the 'Franchise Trust Pact' (CONFIRMED but OLD, dated June 1999): Choice pledged QA reviews are 'conducted in a fair and impartial manner,' gives failing properties 'an opportunity to fix deficiencies and the right to appeal for reinspection,' commits to 'at least 12 months notice' before non-renewal, and in some cases offers a 90-day action plan to preclude non-renewal. CAUTION: this is a 1999 pledge — the cure/appeal SPIRIT persists in current franchise agreements, but specific terms should be confirmed against today's FDD, not quoted as current policy. (hospitalitynet 4002310, 1999)
- FEE STRUCTURE (Comfort Suites, 2024-2025, CONFIRMED): affiliation fee $500/room new ($50,000 min) or $750/room transfer/renewal ($65,000 min), plus a $5,000 non-refundable application fee (credited to affiliation). Royalty is 6.0% of gross room revenue; marketing/ad fund ~3.5-4%. Re-inspection, non-compliance, and guest-satisfaction fees are real but their exact dollar amounts live only in the FDD 'Item 6 — Other Fees' table (FranChimp/Vetted Biz host these but are paywall/Cloudflare-gated; dollar figures NOT retrieved). (vettedbiz Comfort Suites 2024; SEC 10-K)
- SYSTEM SIZE TODAY (CONFIRMED, fresh): As of Dec 31, 2024, Choice has over 7,500 hotels and 650,000+ rooms across 46 countries and territories, 22 brands. (Choice Q4/FY2024 results, Feb 20, 2025)
- WHO RUNS QA (PARTIALLY CONFIRMED — research had an error): Choice outsourced domestic QA to LRA Worldwide starting 2006 across 4,100+ properties (objective third-party model with hotel-ops consultants). CORRECTION: LRA Worldwide was later acquired by DELOITTE ('LRA by Deloitte') — NOT by Coyle. Coyle Hospitality is a SEPARATE, independent QA/mystery-shopping firm. There is NO public confirmation of who runs Choice's US QA today (the LRA source is from 2006); the current delivery model is unverified. (media.choicehotels.com 2006; Deloitte/LRA acquisition release)
- QUALIFIED VENDOR PROGRAM — what it is (CONFIRMED): a vetted directory of approved product/service suppliers offered to franchisees, with brand-negotiated discounts and simplified hotel-level purchasing. Quore (hotel ops/workflow software) was designated a Qualified Vendor on Sept 22, 2016 across ~5,200 US franchised hotels, with a negotiated brand discount off Quore's standard licensing — the closest direct SOFTWARE precedent for Staxis. CORRECTION: this was 2016, NOT 2024. Other approvals: SONIFI (Jul 2017 US, again Feb 2024 US+Canada), Nomadix (Apr 18, 2023). (hospitalitytech/hotelmanagement Quore; sonifi; nomadix)
- QUALIFIED VENDOR — how to get listed (CONFIRMED verbatim from Choice's own page): apply via the Choice Vendor Portal. Application is exactly '9 pages and 140 fields,' can be saved and resumed, '4 to 6 weeks' evaluation, written decision; approved vendors must comply with Choice's Supplier Code of Conduct. Support: procurement_help@choicehotels.com. Six evaluation criteria, quoted: (1) national distribution or service, (2) competitive pricing, (3) strong experience with + excellent references from hospitality clients, (4) proven track record + financial stability, (5) ability to take orders directly from 'over 6,000 hotels via the Internet,' (6) standards and systems for continuous improvement. (apps.choicecentral.com/choicebuys/about/vendors.asp; VendorPortal)

## Competitor teardown: AI-native operations platforms targeting select/limited-service hotels (Staxis's wedge) — fact-checked June 2026

**Bottom line:** The "hotelops.ai" name collision is confirmed NOT the threat — it's a small, no-disclosed-funding Kochi (India) housekeeping/checklist app with Indian/boutique customers and no US-franchise focus. The real direct competitor is Lance (YC W26, San Francisco, ~$5M seed, 50+ hotels across Marriott/Hilton/Hyatt) — it uses the exact same wedge as Staxis: vision-based computer-use AI agents that drive legacy hotel PMS/CRS with no API. But Lance skews to guest-comms/sales/front-desk at larger branded groups, NOT the solo economy-franchise owner Staxis serves. Levee (Chicago, pre-seed, vision-based) and Jurny (LA, ~$16M, but rentals/boutique) round out the field. The biggest medium-term squeeze is the franchisors themselves now shipping free brand-level AI to owners (Choice's CHARLIE/RAISE on AWS, May 2026; Wyndham's 250 live agents, Nov 2025).

### Findings
- NAME-COLLISION (hotelops.ai / HotelOpsAI) — CONFIRMED, different animal. Based in Kochi, Kerala, India (Infopark, Kakkanad). Automates housekeeping, maintenance, guest services with manager + housekeeper apps and dynamic checklists/SOPs. Site claims: 70% fewer service calls, 20-25% productivity boost, 10-15% more operating profit, 60% fewer complaints, 90 min/day saved on scheduling. Customer logos: CGH, Novotel Living, IBNI, Gracle, Mystic Mayapott (Indian/boutique). NO founders, team, or funding disclosed anywhere — India phone + sales@hotelops.ai only; almost certainly bootstrapped. ## Limited-service hotel ROI/pricing dollar benchmarks for Staxis (fact-checked, 2024-2026)

**Bottom line:** The core ROI numbers hold up and are now sharper with fresh Q1 2026 data: a select-service hotel spends about $28-30 in housekeeping labor per occupied room, total labor CPOR jumped 12.8% in one year ($42.82 to $48.32) and accelerated to +21% in Q4 2025, replacing one frontline employee costs roughly $5,500-7,600, hospitality turnover runs ~70-74% a year with 55% of room attendants gone in 90 days, and Cornell's research rigorously shows a 1-point review gain buys an 11.2% price increase with no occupancy loss. Three numbers needed correcting (the Cornell turnover figures were mislabeled, the overtime stat was understated, and the toiletries cost couldn't be verified), but the ROI story is intact: anchor on labor, turnover, and review-driven pricing, not amenities.

### Findings
- HOUSEKEEPING CPOR (confirmed exactly, HotelData H2 2024/Q1 2025): housekeeping labor per occupied room ran $28.28 select-service, $26.29 extended-stay, $57.59 full-service, $123.60 resorts (all-category avg $44.62). FRESHER: select-service total labor CPOR rose to $30.36 in Q1 2026 (up 2.5% from $29.62). This is the housekeeping budget Staxis shrinks.
- TOTAL LABOR CPOR (confirmed exactly, HotelData): full-year average rose from $42.82 (2024) to $48.32 (2025), +12.8% YoY. Q4 2025 wage CPOR alone rose 21.1% YoY (more than double the full-year pace). Full-service hit $73.53 in Q4 2025 (up from $59.39, +23.8%). Labor is the fastest-rising line on the hotel P&L.
- MINUTES/ROOMS PER SHIFT (confirmed, industry standards): ~20-35 min for a checkout/departure clean, ~15-25 min for a stayover. Limited-service attendants target ~16-20 rooms per 8-hour shift (full-service 12-16). Hours per occupied room (HotelData): select-service ~1.48 (down to 1.479 in Q1 2026), extended-stay 1.33, full-service 2.60, resorts 4.88. Every minute saved per room scales: 2 min off 16 rooms = ~32 min/day of labor.
- LABOR AS % OF REVENUE (confirmed, CBRE 2023 Trends, 2,456 hotels): rose from 31.4% to 32.4% of revenue (and 50.9% to 51.7% of total expenses). Rule of thumb: labor is 30-40% of hotel revenue and ~half of operating expense. NOTE: the 31.4->32.4% figure is 2023 data, the latest CBRE Trends release, not 2024.
- OVERTIME TREND (corrected and sharpened, HotelData 2025): overtime as a SHARE OF HOURS climbed Jan-Sept 2025: room attendants 1.49%->1.67% (+12%), laundry attendants 1.22%->1.54% (+25.6%), housekeeping housepersons 1.26%->1.51% (+19.7%). The original draft's '+4.9% YoY' for room-attendant overtime could not be confirmed; the +12% Jan-Sept figure is the well-sourced one. Q1 2026 continued: laundry +13.6%, room attendants +10.3%, housepersons +9.9%. Overtime is creeping up specifically in housekeeping.
- TURNOVER RATE (confirmed): leisure & hospitality runs the highest turnover of any U.S. sector, ~70-74% annually (roughly 5x the 12-15% economy-wide average). ~55% of room attendants quit within their first 90 days; ~40% of all turnover happens in the first year (SHRM). A recurring, predictable cost Staxis can attack.
- COST TO REPLACE ONE EMPLOYEE (CORRECTED): Cornell study of 33 U.S. hotels found total turnover cost averaging $7,612 at INDEPENDENT hotels and $6,957 at CHAIN hotels (the original draft had these labels SWAPPED and cited a '$5,864 average' that could not be confirmed). Lost productivity alone exceeded $4,000 (47-68% of total cost). SHRM 2025 benchmark: $5,475 per non-executive hire (up from $4,700). Gallup: frontline replacement = ~40% of annual salary (~$12K on a $30K housekeeper). Use a defensible range of ~$5,500-7,600 per frontline replacement.
- FRONTLINE WAGES (confirmed, BLS May 2024 + Salary.com 2025-26): maids/housekeeping cleaners median $16.66/hr (BLS); hotel-housekeeping-specific averages ~$15/hr (~$30,308/yr) with a $13-17 range by location. Front-desk agent ~$16/hr (~$33-36K/yr). These rates convert saved minutes and avoided overtime into dollars.
- COST OF A BAD REVIEW (confirmed with key caveat): one cited vendor model puts a single negative review at $15,494/month in lost revenue ASSUMING a $125 ADR, and four bad reviews at ~$59,534/month. A single negative review can drive away ~30 prospective customers (Convergys). Treat the dollar figure as illustrative (vendor model, ADR-dependent), not a hard benchmark.
- REVIEW UPSIDE (confirmed exactly, Cornell / Chris Anderson, the rigorous source): a 1-point gain on a 5-point review scale (e.g. 3.3 to 4.3) = +11.2% ADR with NO loss of occupancy/market share; a 1-point gain on the 100-point Global Review Index = up to +0.89% ADR, +0.54% occupancy, +1.42% RevPAR, across all channels online and offline. This is the strongest, most defensible number in the set.
- GUEST SUPPLY / AMENITY COST (DOWNGRADED - could not verify): the specific '$0.14 per amenity set' and 'Marriott ~$25M/yr' figures could NOT be confirmed. Available sources show hotels pay ~$0.05-0.10 per bar of soap at scale, and Marriott's soap spend is estimated at $72-145M/yr (not $25M). The directional point still holds: per-room amenity cost is small (cents to low single dollars) versus labor, so the ROI story should NOT lean on amenities.
- COMP'D ROOM COST (no public benchmark - model it): no single authoritative figure exists; derive it as room-department CPOR (~$28-30 labor for select-service + a few dollars of supplies) PLUS lost ADR. For a Comfort Suites (upper-midscale), midscale ADR runs ~$90-150, so a single comp commonly costs ~$100-180 in combined out-of-pocket plus foregone revenue. Compute live in the calculator rather than quoting one source.
- CHAIN SCALE (open question RESOLVED): Comfort Suites sits in the UPPER-MIDSCALE chain-scale bucket within Choice Hotels' portfolio. For operational labor benchmarking, select-service (and secondarily extended-stay) remain the right CPOR/HPOR proxies.
